## Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. The Golden Rule (ChatGPT)
"It's all about how you ask. The more clearly you describe what you want, the better the result."

**Before/After Pattern:**
- Vague: "Write an email about the delay"
- Better: "Act as a project manager. Write a professional yet reassuring email to a client named Sarah about a 5-day delay due to supplier issues. Keep it concise, polite, and include new estimated timeline."

### 2. Extended Thinking Mode (Claude)
Framed as taking "a few extra seconds to think through a question step by step, checking assumptions, weighing alternatives, and connecting patterns across long documents."

**Use Cases:**
- Avoids "shallow one-line summaries on complex topics"
- Planning, data interpretation, comparing multiple inputs
- Reduces AI hallucinations by showing structured logic
- Best for: reports, meeting notes, policies that need analysis (not just summary)

### 3. First Draft Generator (ChatGPT)
"Think of ChatGPT as your first draft generator" - emphasis on iteration and refinement rather than final outputs.

**Process:**
1. Generate outline
2. Expand specific sections
3. Refine tone/style
4. Edit as needed

### 4. Data Sensitivity Protocol
"ChatGPT doesn't know who you are, but you still shouldn't paste confidential info."

**Best Practice:** Replace names and numbers with placeholders in square brackets BEFORE pasting into AI.

### 5. Tool Integration Philosophy
"Save successful prompts, iterate outputs, and integrate with tools like Notion, Docs, or Excel to maximize value."

AI is positioned as part of a productivity stack, not a standalone solution.

## Language & Explanatory Style

### Tone Characteristics
- **Practical, not theoretical** - "Not as a magic wand, but as a practical, powerful tool"
- **Conversational** - "Here's the deal," "Let me show you," "You will love it"
- **Reassuring** - Acknowledges common frustrations ("spending half a day writing the same kind of things over and over")
- **Directive** - Clear instructions with action verbs ("Try this," "Here's what you can do")

### Accessibility Techniques
1. **Relatable scenarios** - Project manager emailing about delays, not abstract examples
2. **Before/after comparisons** - Shows bad vs. good prompts side by side
3. **Time references** - "In seconds," "12 seconds to think it through" (builds trust in tool speed)
4. **Specific numbers** - "5-day delay," "700-word article," "10% price increase" (concrete, not vague)
5. **Role-based framing** - "If you're a student or a manager," "as a business professional, freelancer, job seeker"

### Teaching Patterns
- **Problem → Solution** - "You're bouncing between emails... this is where ChatGPT comes in"
- **Show the interface** - Describes clicking through menus, selecting options
- **Progressive disclosure** - Basic feature → Advanced use case
- **Safety warnings** - "Here's the one you should be especially concerned about" (data sensitivity)

### Metaphors & Analogies
- "Hidden superpower" (extended thinking)
- "Magic wand" (what AI is NOT)
- "Tone switch in your writing"
- "Free, always-on career coach"
- "First draft generator"

## Gaps & Course Opportunities

### What's Missing

1. **No philosophical framework** - Video is pure tactics, no "why AI matters" or "how to think about AI"
   - **Opportunity:** Position course around "What matters most in the age of AI is..." series

2. **No courage/mindset component** - Assumes viewer will just start using AI
   - **Opportunity:** Address fear, impostor syndrome, "am I cheating?" concerns

3. **No workflow design** - Shows individual features but not how to build systematic AI habits
   - **Opportunity:** Teach "AI as organizational layer" (your invoicing tool, photo renaming, chief of staff examples)

4. **Limited personalization** - Generic use cases (project manager, student)
   - **Opportunity:** Help people find THEIR use cases through exercises/worksheets

5. **No creative applications** - All examples are business/productivity focused
   - **Opportunity:** Song creation, creative writing, brainstorming, personal projects

6. **Surface-level prompting** - "Golden rule" is good but not deep
   - **Opportunity:** Advanced prompt engineering, chain-of-thought, meta-prompting

7. **Tool comparison but no decision framework** - Shows both but doesn't help viewer choose
   - **Opportunity:** Decision matrix for when to use what (Claude for X, ChatGPT for Y)

8. **No automation/scaling** - Everything is one-off queries
   - **Opportunity:** Custom GPTs, API usage, building AI tools (your blog publishing workflow)

9. **No ethical considerations** - Only mentions data sensitivity
   - **Opportunity:** Attribution, over-reliance, maintaining human judgment

10. **No follow-up resources** - Video ends with "watch this next"
    - **Opportunity:** Worksheets, prompt libraries, community for sharing use cases

## Course Design Implications

### What to Keep (Proven to Work)
1. **Show, don't tell** - Real examples, real outputs, real time estimates
2. **Before/after** - Bad prompt → good prompt comparisons are instantly valuable
3. **Specific scenarios** - "Project manager with delay" beats "write better emails"
4. **Safety emphasis** - People DO worry about data/privacy; address it early
5. **Iteration messaging** - "First draft, not final answer" reduces pressure

### What to Evolve (Your Differentiation)
1. **Add "why"** - Not just "how to prompt" but "why this matters for your life/work"
2. **Add courage** - Permission, mindset, overcoming resistance
3. **Add personalization** - Help people find THEIR use cases, not generic ones
4. **Add creativity** - Go beyond business productivity into personal projects
5. **Add community** - Turn individual learning into collective discovery
