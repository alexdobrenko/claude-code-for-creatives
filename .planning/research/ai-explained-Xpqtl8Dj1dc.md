## Gaps & Opportunities for AI Course

### What's Missing from This Explanation:

1. **HOW to actually use it effectively**
   - No prompt engineering tips
   - No examples of good vs. bad prompts
   - No demonstration of features in action
   - Just "sign up and ask questions"

2. **Strategic thinking about AI tools**
   - When to use which tool (mentioned but not deeply explored)
   - How to combine tools for workflows
   - Cost/benefit analysis of free vs. paid

3. **Practical workflows**
   - No step-by-step examples
   - No templates or frameworks to apply
   - No "here's what to do Monday morning" guidance

4. **Depth on capabilities**
   - Very surface-level feature overview
   - No examples of complex tasks
   - No demonstration of quality differences

5. **Common mistakes/pitfalls**
   - No discussion of limitations
   - No guidance on verifying outputs
   - No tips for getting better results

6. **Integration with existing work**
   - How does this fit into current workflows?
   - What tools does it replace vs. complement?
   - How to transition from current methods?

## Key Takeaways for Course Design

1. **Start where this video ends:** Assume people know Claude exists, show them how to actually use it
2. **Lead with outcomes, not features:** "Save 5 hours/week on research" vs. "Claude can read long documents"
3. **Provide decision frameworks:** When to use which tool, not just what each tool does
4. **Show real workflows:** Your actual examples (invoicing, blog publishing, etc.)
5. **Address the courage gap:** People know AI exists but don't know how to start integrating it
6. **Focus on integration:** How AI fits into existing work, not replacing everything

### Course Positioning Statement (using Yasin framework):

"I help **knowledge workers and content creators** get **10+ hours back per week** without **learning to code or spending thousands on tools** using **practical AI workflows that integrate with their current systems**."

**Enemy:** Surface-level AI tutorials that explain what tools do but not how to actually use them in your specific work.

**Belief:** AI is most valuable when integrated into your existing workflows, not when it replaces everything you do.
