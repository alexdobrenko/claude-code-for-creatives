## Overview

Greg Isenberg walks through a complete 6-step framework for building a startup using AI tools, demonstrating how to replace a developer, salesperson, designer, marketer, researcher, and product manager with AI agents. He actually builds a product (LLM Boost - an AI SEO agency tool) live during the episode.

---

## The 6-Step Framework

### Step 1: Find an Idea (Based on Trends)
- **Tool:** ideabrowser.com (Greg's own product)
- Daily AI-generated startup ideas with trend analysis
- Includes opportunity score, problem score, market analysis
- "Founder fit score" feature to assess if YOU should pursue the idea
- Key insight: "Build an idea based on a trend because it's easier"

### Step 2: Sketch Out the Idea
- **Tool:** TLDraw (tldraw.com) or FigJam
- Quick wireframing/sketching (7 minutes)
- Critical tip: "When you give an LLM an image, you're going to get better results"
- Makes it easier for non-designers to communicate vision

### Step 3: Scope Out the MVP
- **Tool:** Manus (manus.im) - "Chinese but extremely good"
- AI product manager that creates full project plans
- Goes beyond ChatGPT - actively researches by surfing the web
- Can watch it work in real-time and provide feedback
- Key prompt technique: "Ask me any questions before you get started" (gets better output)

### Step 4: Vibe Code a Prototype
- **Tool:** Bolt.new (primary), also mentions Lovable, Windsurf, Cursor
- Built working prototype in ~4 minutes
- Uses Supabase for backend
- Pro tip: Ask Manus to create the perfect prompt for your AI developer first

### Step 5: Vibe Market the Business
- **Tools:** Lindy AI, The Vibe Marketer, Prospect AI, Boring Marketing, Gumloop, N8N
- Automate outbound sales and marketing
- AI SDR (Sales Development Rep) that sends personalized emails
- Can scrape websites, send emails, handle responses automatically

### Step 6: AI Agent Product Manager
- **Tool:** Idea Browser (ideabrowser.com)
- Upload your idea for comprehensive analysis
- Analyzes YouTube, Reddit, and other platforms
- Provides market research, competitor analysis, go-to-market strategies

## Key Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. The "Ask Questions First" Prompt Pattern
- Always end prompts with: "Ask me any questions before you get started"
- Gets better output by letting AI clarify requirements
- Works across all LLMs

### 2. Image-Enhanced Prompting
- Upload sketches/wireframes with prompts
- "I've noticed that when you give an LLM an image, you're going to get better results"
- Even rough sketches (7 minutes) make huge difference

### 3. Persona-Based Consulting
- "Pretend you're Warren Buffett - how would he solve this?"
- "Pretend you're a BCG/McKinsey consultant who's cold-hearted and all about operations"
- Upload books/content from people you admire for their POV

### 4. Context Stacking
- Upload courses, PDFs, documents for additional context
- Example: Uploaded LLM SEO mini-course to Manus
- "The point is you can paste anything into here for context"

### 5. The Six-Step Startup Framework
1. Find idea (trend-based)
2. Sketch it (visual wireframe)
3. Scope MVP (AI product manager)
4. Vibe code prototype (AI developer)
5. Vibe market (AI SDR/marketer)
6. AI agent product manager (ongoing)

### 6. Idea Validation Framework (from Idea Browser)
- **Opportunity Score** - Market size/potential
- **Problem Score** - Pain point severity
- **Founder Fit Score** - Are YOU the right person?
- Checks skill alignment, resource availability, market positioning

### 7. "Learning in Public" Philosophy
- Greg uses podcast as opportunity to learn
- "I like about the podcast... it's an opportunity to actually learn in public"
- Teaching is learning

## Gaps & Opportunities for an AI Course

### 1. **The "How to Stay Current" Problem**
Sam asks multiple times: "How do you stay on top of all of this?"
- Greg's answer: "I'm a nerd" and "following tweets" isn't actionable
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Teach a system for discovering new tools
- Create a "reading the trades" framework (who to follow, where to look)
- Weekly/monthly tool discovery process

### 2. **The Prompting Skills Gap**
Greg shows advanced prompting but doesn't teach the fundamentals:
- Why does "ask me questions first" work?
- How to structure multi-step prompts
- When to use images vs. text vs. documents
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Comprehensive prompt engineering curriculum
- Beginner → Intermediate → Advanced prompting
- Platform-specific techniques (ChatGPT vs. Claude vs. Manus)

### 3. **The Tool Selection Problem**
Too many tools mentioned without decision framework:
- Bolt vs. Lovable vs. Cursor vs. Windsurf?
- Lindy vs. Gumloop vs. N8N?
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Teach tool selection criteria
- When to use what tool for what job
- Cost/benefit analysis framework
- "Your AI stack" based on use case

### 4. **The "Nerd Tax" Issue**
Greg says "you can't outsource this" but doesn't explain how non-nerds catch up:
- Sam feels overwhelmed but motivated
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Accelerated learning path for executives/non-technical founders
- "AI for the rest of us" positioning
- Focus on practical business outcomes, not tool mastery

### 5. **The Verification/Quality Gap**
No discussion of:
- How to verify AI output is correct
- When AI hallucinates or makes mistakes
- Quality control processes
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Teach critical evaluation of AI work
- Verification workflows
- When to trust AI vs. when to double-check

### 6. **The Ethics/Security Gap**
Greg mentions Manus is Chinese: "Be careful... I'm not uploading my financial data"
- But no framework for data security
- **OPPORTUNITY**: AI security & privacy curriculum
- What data to never upload
- Tool trustworthiness evaluation
- Self-hosting vs. cloud considerations

### 7. **The Integration Problem**
Shows individual tools but not how they connect:
- How does Idea Browser output flow into Manus?
- How does Manus output flow into Bolt?
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Teach complete workflow integration
- Tool chain strategies
- Data handoff between tools
- Automation pipelines

### 8. **The "Beyond the Demo" Reality**
Builds a landing page in 4 minutes but:
- What about bugs?
- What about scaling?
- What about maintenance?
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Post-MVP curriculum
- Debugging AI-generated code
- Iterating with AI
- Going from prototype to production

### 9. **The Voice-to-Text Advantage**
Greg mentions Wispr Flow as "my favorite AI tool" but:
- Doesn't explain setup
- Doesn't show workflows
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Voice-first AI interaction course
- Voice prompting techniques
- Voice + AI productivity stack
- Accessibility angle

### 10. **The ROI/Metrics Gap**
Claims about replacing employees but no measurement:
- How much time actually saved?
- What's the quality difference?
- What are the costs?
- **OPPORTUNITY**: AI ROI tracking framework
- Before/after metrics
- Cost analysis (time + tools + errors)
- Business case templates

2. Add: "Ask me any questions before you get started"
3. AI generates clarifying questions
4. User responds (can use Wispr Flow for voice)

## Final Thoughts

This episode is a masterclass in demonstrating AI capability, but it's NOT a course. It's inspiration without instruction, tools without technique, results without roadmap.

The gap between "watch Greg do it" and "I can do it myself" is HUGE - and that's where your course lives.

Key tensions to address:
- **Speed vs. Quality** - 4 minutes is impressive, but is it production-ready?
- **Nerd vs. Non-Nerd** - Can't outsource, but not everyone has time to "play with tools"
- **Tools vs. Skills** - Manus is great, but what if it shuts down tomorrow?
- **Individual vs. Team** - Greg can learn this, but how does Sam teach 50 employees?

**Your positioning**: "The systematic path from AI-curious to AI-powered, without becoming a full-time nerd."
